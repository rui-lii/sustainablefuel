The Future of Transportation

Why sustainable fuels are inevitable

Why do we need cars that run on renewable fuels?

Cars that run on renewable energies must be implemented soon. It is one of the most impactful climate change factor.

Electricity

Electric vehicles can draw electricity directly from the grid and other off-board electrical power sources and store it in batteries. Using electricity to power vehicles can have significant energy and emissions benefits.

Biodiesel/Ethanol

Biodiesel is a domestically produced, renewable fuel that can be manufactured from vegetable oils, or animal fats for use in diesel vehicles. Most importantly, biodiesel burns cleaner than petroleum.

Hydrogen

Hydrogen is generally used to generate electricity through a fuel cell. It is an emissions-free alternative fuel that can be produced from domestic energy sources.

Where are we now?


Flex-fuel vehicles are widely available, and it means that the vehicle can run on gasoline or ethanol. However, consumers must realize that the car they own can run on more environmentally friendly fuel and must purchase and use that fuel.


Electric vehicles are currently commercially available, and some are purchasable at affordable and reasonable prices.


Research and commercial efforts are under way to build the hydrogen fueling infrastructure and produce hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicles that are practical for widespread use.

The Keeling Curve

The Keeling Curve, which are measurements conducted in Mauna Loa over many years have shown the amount of CO2 in our atmosphere has been consistently increasing, which has led to increases in our global average temperature. The greenhouse effect that I’m mentioning is when the gases being released increases Earth’s natural greenhouse effect such that more heat is being trapped inside our atmosphere, which in turn increases our global average temperature. This can cause glacial melting, increased sea levels, ocean acidification, agriculture problems, and because of the release of cold water from polar ice melting, it can affect the global ocean conveyor belt (which transports and distributes heat around the Earth). In the US, cars and trucks account for around 1/5 of all U.S. greenhouse gas emissions.

Companies

How can you help?

Write to your politician to implement new emission policies, and to also use cars that run on hybrid fuels, electricity or hydrogen fuel cells. Choose clean fuels over gasoline. Carpool whenever possible, and take public transit to lower your carbon footprint from transportation.
